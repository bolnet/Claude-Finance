**Frontmatter:** Same as walkthrough-accounting.md — description says "Private equity walkthrough -- due diligence scoring, multi-prospect comparison, and portfolio company monitoring". Same allowed-tools list (validate_environment, ingest_csv, investor_classifier, classify_investor). Model: sonnet.

**Scenario framing:** You are a VP at a mid-market PE fund evaluating three acquisition prospects for Fund III. The fund has a portfolio of existing companies that need quarterly monitoring. The scenario uses demo/sample_portfolio.csv as the fund's proprietary prospect database exported from the CRM.

**Structure — 4 phases, 11-13 steps total:**

**Phase 1: Pre-Flight Check (1 step)**
- Step 1: validate_environment — same pattern as all walkthroughs. Frame: "Before beginning due diligence, the deal team validates that the analytical environment is ready. A PE fund's quantitative screening depends on pandas for data manipulation, scikit-learn for prospect scoring models, and matplotlib for portfolio reporting."

**Phase 2: Deal Pipeline Profiling (3 steps)**
- Step 2: ingest_csv with csv_path="demo/sample_portfolio.csv" (no target_column) — full data profile. Frame as CRM export review: columns mapped to PE terms (credit_score = creditworthiness for debt capacity assessment, debt_ratio = leverage profile for LBO modeling, income = revenue proxy for target sizing, risk_tolerance = management risk appetite indicator, segment = current fund allocation bucket: conservative=value/buyout, moderate=growth equity, aggressive=venture/high-growth). Note row count as "deal pipeline contains [N] prospects."
- Step 3: ingest_csv with csv_path="demo/sample_portfolio.csv", target_column="segment" — segment distribution. Frame as fund allocation analysis: "The segment distribution reveals how the deal pipeline is distributed across investment strategies. Conservative = value/buyout candidates, Moderate = growth equity candidates, Aggressive = venture/high-growth candidates. A balanced pipeline indicates diversified deal sourcing; heavy skew toward one segment suggests the fund's sourcing network is biased."
- Step 4: Pure analysis step (NO tool call) — Deal Pipeline Summary Report. Compile Steps 2-3 into a PE-framed data quality report with sections: Pipeline Size, Data Completeness, Strategy Distribution, Sourcing Assessment. Frame as what the deal team would present at Monday's investment committee meeting.

**Phase 3: Due Diligence Scoring Model (5 steps)**
- Step 5: investor_classifier with csv_path="demo/sample_portfolio.csv" — train scoring model. Frame: "The deal team trains a classification model on the historical prospect database to establish a quantitative scoring baseline. Model accuracy represents the consistency of historical fund allocation decisions — a high accuracy means the fund's investment committee has been consistent in how it categorizes prospects. Feature importances reveal which prospect characteristics most influence the allocation decision."
- Step 6: Pure analysis step (NO tool call) — Feature Importance as Due Diligence Priorities. Interpret feature importances as due diligence priorities: the most important features are the fields the deal team should scrutinize first during prospect evaluation. Frame in PE language: "If risk_tolerance is the top feature, the deal team's primary due diligence question for every prospect is: What is management's appetite for operational transformation?"
- Step 7: classify_investor — Prospect A (Growth Equity Profile): age=42, income=145000, risk_tolerance=0.55, product_preference="equities". Frame as: "Prospect A is a mid-career executive leading a $145K revenue business with moderate risk appetite and equity-oriented capital structure. The scoring model classifies this prospect as [segment] with [X]% confidence. In PE terms: [if moderate] this is a textbook growth equity candidate — established business, moderate risk, room for operational value creation. [if other] the model suggests this prospect may be better suited for [segment] strategy."
- Step 8: classify_investor — Prospect B (Buyout/Value Profile): age=58, income=190000, risk_tolerance=0.25, product_preference="bonds". Frame as: "Prospect B is a seasoned operator running a $190K revenue business with low risk appetite and debt-oriented capital structure. The deal team expects a conservative/buyout classification — this profile maps to a leveraged buyout candidate with stable cash flows and low operational risk. Confidence score indicates how cleanly this prospect fits the buyout archetype."
- Step 9: classify_investor — Prospect C (Venture/High-Growth Profile): age=26, income=72000, risk_tolerance=0.88, product_preference="stocks". Frame as: "Prospect C is an early-stage operator with a $72K revenue business, very high risk appetite, and equity-oriented structure. The deal team expects an aggressive/venture classification — this is a high-growth, high-risk profile that typically requires minority equity investment rather than a control buyout."

**Phase 4: Portfolio Monitoring & Investment Committee Report (3-4 steps)**
- Step 10: Pure analysis step (NO tool call) — Multi-Prospect Comparison Table. Build a side-by-side comparison table of Prospects A, B, C with columns: Prospect, Age, Revenue (Income), Risk Appetite, Capital Structure, Model Classification, Confidence, Recommended Strategy, Investment Thesis Summary. Frame as the deal screening matrix the investment committee reviews. Rank by model confidence — highest confidence = strongest conviction. Identify which prospect has the weakest fit (lowest confidence) and recommend additional due diligence.
- Step 11: Pure analysis step (NO tool call) — Portfolio Company Monitoring Dashboard. Reinterpret the three classify_investor results as existing portfolio companies rather than prospects. Frame as quarterly portfolio review: "For existing portfolio companies, the classifier confidence score serves as a portfolio health indicator. A company originally acquired as a 'growth equity' play whose profile now scores with low confidence for that segment is showing drift — the operating characteristics have changed, and the fund should reassess the value creation plan." Build a monitoring table: Company, Original Thesis, Current Classification, Confidence, Drift Signal (Yes/No), Recommended Action.
- Step 12: Pure synthesis step (NO tool call) — Investment Committee Memo. Compile all findings into an IC memo with sections:
  1. Deal Pipeline Health (Phase 2 findings)
  2. Scoring Model Reliability (Phase 3 accuracy + feature importance)
  3. Prospect Screening Results (Phase 3 classifications)
  4. Portfolio Monitoring Alerts (Phase 4 drift signals)
  5. Recommended Actions (prioritized list)
  Frame the bottom line: "Based on the scoring model's [X]% accuracy and the prospect classifications, the deal team recommends [advancing/tabling/declining] each prospect. Portfolio monitoring identifies [N] companies showing classification drift that warrant board-level review."

**Closing section:** "What a PE Fund Would Do Next" — 4-5 bullets covering: financial model construction (3-statement + LBO), management presentation prep, term sheet drafting for highest-conviction prospect, quarterly board deck updates for portfolio drift companies.

**Final line and disclaimer:** Same pattern as all walkthroughs — "Walkthrough complete. Use `/finance` for ad-hoc analysis." + "For educational/informational purposes only. Not financial advice."
